Oprah Winfrey

One of the most recognised TV personalities in the world today.

Here's a timeline of Oprah Winfrey's accomplishments to date:

  • 1954 - Born on January 29,1954 in Mississippi. Her father Vernon Winfrey was a coal miner. Her mother Vernita Lee was a housemaid.
  • 1954-60 - Oprah lived with her grandmother, Hattie Mae Lee on a farm.
  • 1960-67 - At the age of six, she moved in with her single mother. She experienced considerable hardship during her childhood, including being raped at the age of nine and becoming pregnant at 14; her son died in infancy.
  • 1968 - Her mother sent her to live with her father in Nashville, Tennessee. Her father was strict and made her education a priority. She became an honors student and graduated from East Nashville High School.
  • 1972 - Oprah won the Miss Black Tennessee beauty pageant. The local black radio station hired her to do the news part-time.
  • 1973 - Oprah graduated from Tennessee State University with a degree in Speech and Performing Arts. She became the first black TV news anchor.
  • 1976 - Oprah moved to Baltimore to co-anchor the six o’clock news.
  • 1983 - Oprah moved to Chicago to host WLS-TV’s low-rated half-hour morning talk-show, AM Chicago.
  • 1984 - Within months after she took over, the show went from last place in the ratings to overtaking Donahue as the highest rated talk show in Chicago. It was renamed as “The Oprah Winfrey Show”.
  • 1986 - The Oprah Winfrey Show was expanded to a full hour, and broadcast nationally.
  • 1988 - The International Television and Radio Society named Oprah Broadcaster of the Year.
  • 1991 - Oprah testified before the US congress and initiated the National Child Protection Act.
  • 1992 - Oprah was engaged to Stedman Graham, a public relations executive. She also won the Best talk show Host Emmy award.
  • 1993 - Oprah hosted a rare prime-time interview with Michael Jackson which became the fourth most watched event in American television history.
  • 1994 - Oprah won Best Talk Show and Best Talk Show Host at the 21st Annual Daytime Emmy Awards.
  • 1995 - Won an Emmy for Best Talk Show and Best Host for the second year in a row.
  • 1998 - Became a co-author of "Journey to Beloved" and received a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Daytime Emmy Awards and an award for top talk show host.
  • 2002 - Oprah became the recipient of the first Bob Hope Humanitarian Award at the 2002 Emmy Awards for services to television and film.
  • 2005 - Oprah was named by Time as one of the 100 most influential people of the year.
  • 2011 - Oprah launched her own cable network, the Oprah Winfrey Network, or OWN.
